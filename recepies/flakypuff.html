  • Classic Flaky Puff Pastry

  • Puff pastry is a buttery, delicate dough that bakes into crisp, golden layers. Made by folding and rolling butter into flour, it creates a light, airy texture perfect for both sweet and savory treats. From croissants to tarts, its versatility and rich flavor make it a baking essential!

  • Fudgy brownies are rich, dense, and indulgently moist treats that have a soft, gooey center and a slightly crisp top. Unlike cake-like brownies, which are lighter and fluffier, fudgy brownies have a compact, almost fudgy texture, thanks to the high ratio of butter and sugar to flour. The perfect fudgy brownie should melt in your mouth, delivering a deep, intense chocolate flavor with a rich, velvety finish. When cut, they should hold their shape but feel moist and almost sticky in the center, with small cracks forming on the top during baking. They're the ultimate chocolate lover’s dream – decadent, chewy, and irresistibly delicious!

  • Ingridients:

  • 2 ½ cups (315g) all-purpose flour

  • 1 tsp salt

  • 1 cup (225g) unsalted butter, cold and cubed

  • ½ cup (120ml) cold water

  • 1 tsp lemon juice (optional, for better elasticity)

  • Instructions:

  • 1. In a bowl, mix flour and salt.

  • 2. Add cold cubed butter and use a pastry cutter (or your hands) to break it into small pea-sized pieces.

  • 3. Slowly add cold water and lemon juice, mixing until a rough dough forms. Do not overwork it.

  • 4. Shape into a disc, wrap in plastic, and refrigerate for 30 minutes.

  • 5. Lightly flour a surface and roll the dough into a 12x6-inch rectangle.

  • 6. Fold it into thirds (like a letter). Turn 90° and roll out again. Repeat this process 6 times (this creates layers).

  • 7. Chill the dough for 30 minutes between every 2 folds to keep the butter cold.

  • 8. After the final fold, wrap and refrigerate for at least 1 hour (or overnight for better results).

  • 9. Roll out, cut, and use for croissants, turnovers, or puff pastry tarts.

  • 10. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 18-22 minutes until golden and puffy.

  • Pro tip: For extra crispiness, brush pastries with an egg wash (1 egg + 1 tbsp milk) before baking.
