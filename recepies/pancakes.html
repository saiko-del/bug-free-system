  • Keto Coconut Flour Pancakes (Gluten-Free, Dairy-Free)

  • Fluffy, light, and packed with flavor, these keto-friendly pancakes are a perfect breakfast option!

  • These keto coconut flour pancakes are a delicious and healthy alternative to traditional pancakes. Made with coconut flour, these pancakes are low-carb, gluten-free, and dairy-free, making them suitable for a variety of dietary needs. Fluffy on the inside with a slightly crisp edge, these pancakes are perfect when topped with sugar-free syrup, fresh berries, or your favorite keto-friendly toppings.

  • Ingredients:

  • ¼ cup coconut flour

  • 4 large eggs

  • ¼ cup unsweetened almond milk (or other dairy-free milk)

  • 2 tbsp melted coconut oil or avocado oil

  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

  • 1 tsp baking powder

  • ¼ tsp salt

  • 1-2 tbsp sweetener (such as stevia, monk fruit, or erythritol, optional)

  • Instructions:

  • 1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, almond milk, melted coconut oil, and vanilla extract.

  • 2. In a separate bowl, mix the coconut flour, baking powder, salt, and sweetener (if using).

  • 3. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, stirring until the batter is smooth and well combined. Let the batter sit for 2-3 minutes to thicken.

  • 4. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium-low heat and lightly grease with coconut oil or cooking spray.

  • 5. Pour about 2-3 tablespoons of the batter onto the skillet for each pancake. Use the back of the spoon to spread the batter into a small circle.

  • 6. Cook the pancakes for 2-3 minutes, or until bubbles form on the surface and the edges begin to look set. Flip the pancakes and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, or until golden brown and cooked through.

  • 7. Remove the pancakes from the skillet and keep warm. Repeat with the remaining batter.

  • 8. Serve the pancakes with your favorite keto-friendly toppings, such as sugar-free syrup, berries, whipped cream, or nuts.

  • Pro Tip: For extra fluffiness, add 1 tbsp of apple cider vinegar to the wet ingredients before mixing with the dry ingredients!
