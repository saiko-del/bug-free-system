  • Classic Butter Biscuits

  • Flaky, buttery, and perfectly golden—these biscuits are a timeless comfort food!

  • Classic butter biscuits are light, fluffy, and rich in buttery flavor. With a crisp golden crust and a soft, tender interior, they make the perfect side for breakfast, soups, or as a snack on their own. Made with simple ingredients and baked to perfection, these biscuits are a staple for any home cook.

  • Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour

  • 1 tbsp baking powder

  • 1 tsp salt

  • 1 tbsp granulated sugar (optional)

  • ½ cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, cold and cubed

  • ¾ cup whole milk or buttermilk

  • Instructions:

  • 1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  • 2. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, salt, and sugar.

  • 3. Cut in the cold butter using a pastry cutter or your fingers until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  • 4. Gradually add milk, stirring gently until the dough comes together.

  • 5. Transfer the dough to a floured surface and knead lightly, then roll it out to about ¾-inch thickness.

  • 6. Use a biscuit cutter or a glass to cut out biscuits and place them on the prepared baking sheet.

  • 7. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until golden brown on top.

  • 8. Remove from the oven and brush with melted butter for extra flavor.

  • Pro Tip: For extra flakiness, fold the dough over itself a few times before rolling it out!

  • Enjoy your warm, homemade butter biscuits!
