  • Apple Turnovers

  • Flaky, golden pastries filled with sweet, cinnamon-spiced apples!

  • Apple turnovers are a delightful handheld pastry featuring a buttery, flaky crust and a warm, cinnamon-spiced apple filling. These delicious treats are easy to make and perfect for breakfast, dessert, or a sweet snack. Serve them with a dusting of powdered sugar or a drizzle of glaze for extra indulgence!

  • Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet of puff pastry, thawed

  • 2 cups apples, peeled, cored, and diced

  • ¼ cup granulated sugar

  • 1 tbsp brown sugar

  • 1 tbsp all-purpose flour

  • ½ tsp cinnamon

  • 1 tbsp lemon juice

  • 1 egg (for egg wash)

  • Powdered sugar or glaze for topping (optional)

  • Instructions:

  • 1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  • 2. In a bowl, mix diced apples, granulated sugar, brown sugar, flour, cinnamon, and lemon juice. Stir well to combine.

  • 3. Roll out the puff pastry and cut it into 4 equal squares.

  • 4. Place a spoonful of the apple filling in the center of each square.

  • 5. Fold each square diagonally to form a triangle, pressing the edges together. Use a fork to seal the edges.

  • 6. Beat the egg and brush it over the turnovers for a golden finish.

  • 7. Bake for 18-22 minutes or until golden brown and crispy.

  • 8. Let cool slightly before dusting with powdered sugar or drizzling with glaze.

  • 9. Enjoy your homemade apple turnovers!
