  • Vegan Croissants (Egg & Dairy-Free)

  • Buttery, flaky, and perfectly golden—these croissants are a vegan dream!

  • These vegan croissants are the perfect balance of light, flaky layers with a buttery flavor, all while being egg and dairy-free. With the right techniques, you can create these delicious, melt-in-your-mouth pastries that are perfect for breakfast, brunch, or as a snack. The layers are achieved by folding and rolling the dough multiple times, creating a beautiful flaky texture that will impress anyone!

  • Ingredients:

  • 3 ½ cups all-purpose flour

  • 1 tbsp sugar

  • 1 ½ tsp salt

  • 1 tbsp active dry yeast

  • 1 cup warm water (110°F/45°C)

  • 1 cup vegan butter (chilled)

  • ½ cup almond milk (or any plant-based milk)

  • 2 tbsp olive oil (or vegetable oil)

  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar

  • Instructions:

  • 1. In a small bowl, combine warm water, yeast, and sugar. Stir and let sit for 5-10 minutes until foamy.

  • 2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the yeast mixture, almond milk, olive oil, and apple cider vinegar. Stir until a dough forms.

  • 3. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 5-7 minutes, until it’s smooth and elastic. Shape it into a ball.

  • 4. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours or until doubled in size.

  • 5. Once the dough has risen, gently punch it down and roll it out into a large rectangle (about 1/4-inch thick).

  • 6. Place the chilled vegan butter between two sheets of parchment paper. Use a rolling pin to flatten the butter into a thin square (about 8x8 inches).

  • 7. Place the butter square in the center of the dough rectangle, and fold the dough over the butter like an envelope (fold the top and bottom edges over the butter, then fold the sides over). Pinch the seams to seal.

  • 8. Roll the dough out again into a long rectangle (about 1/4-inch thick). Fold it into thirds, like a letter, and refrigerate for 30 minutes.

  • 9. Repeat the rolling and folding process 2 more times, refrigerating for 30 minutes between each fold.

  • 10. After the final fold, roll the dough into a large rectangle (about 1/4-inch thick). Cut the dough into triangles and roll each triangle into a croissant shape, starting from the wide end.

  • 11. Place the croissants on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Cover with a cloth and let them rise for 1-2 hours until puffed up.

  • 12. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Bake the croissants for 15-20 minutes, or until golden brown and flaky.

  • 13. Allow the croissants to cool slightly before serving. Enjoy them fresh and warm!

  • Pro Tip: For an extra golden finish, brush the croissants with a little almond milk before baking!
